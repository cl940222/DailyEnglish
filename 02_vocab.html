Vocabulary for Today

根據你的英文能力學習10個英文單字
請選擇一個階級開始學習

opposite

adj.   located or situated on the other side or at a different end of a particular space.
另一邊的，相反的，對面的

▪ My brother and I live on opposite sides of the city.
      我哥哥和我住在城裡兩個方向相反的區。

plural

n.   a word or form that expresses more than one
複數形式

▪ "Woman" in the plural is "women".
      woman 的複數形式是 women。

verb

n.   a word or phrase that describes an action, condition, or experience
動詞

▪ The words "run", "keep", and "feel" are all verbs.
      run，keep 和 feel 都是動詞。

sentence

n.   a group of words, usually containing a verb, that expresses a thought in the form of a statement, question, instruction, or exclamation and starts with a capital letter when written
句，句子

▪ Your conclusion is good, but the final sentence is too long and complicated.
      你的結論沒錯，但最後一句話太長太複雜。

synonym

n.   a word or phrase that has the same or nearly the same meaning as another word or phrase in the same language
同義詞，近義詞

▪ The words "small" and "little" are synonyms.
      small 和 little 是同義詞。

accomodate

v.   to provide with a place to live or to be stored in
為…提供住宿；容納；為…提供空間

▪ There wasn't enough space to accommodate the files.
      沒有足夠的空間存放這些檔案。

week

n.   a period of seven days, especially either from Monday to Sunday or from Sunday to Saturday
星期，週（尤指從星期一到星期日或從星期日到星期六）

▪ It usually takes about four weeks to get the forms processed.
      處理這些表格通常需要四週左右的時間。

spelling

n.   forming words with the correct letters in the correct order, or the ability to do this
拼寫；拼寫能力

▪ My computer has a program which corrects my spelling.
      我的電腦有糾正拼寫錯誤的程式。

capital

n.   a city that is the centre of government of a country or smaller political area
首都；首府；省會

▪ Australia's capital city is Canberra.
      澳大利亞的首都是坎培拉。

present continuous

n.   the verb form used for actions or events that are happening or developing now
現在進行式

▪ The sentences "The kids are watching TV" and "The weather is getting colder" are in the present continuous.
      句子 The children are watching television 和 The weather is getting colder 用的是現在進行式。

ambiguous

adj.   having or expressing more than one possible meaning, sometimes intentionally
含糊不清的；引起歧義的，模棱兩可的；不明確的

▪ His reply to my question was somewhat ambiguous.
      他對我的提問回答得有些含糊不清。

conscientious

adj.   putting a lot of effort into your work
認真的；盡心盡責的；一絲不苟的

▪ Lisa is a conscientious student who always completes her assignments on time.
      麗莎是一個認真的學生，總是按時完成作業。

diligent

adj.   careful and using a lot of effort
認真刻苦的，勤奮的，勤勞的

▪ Their lawyer was extremely diligent in preparing their case.
      律師為他們這個案子做了認真紮實的準備。

impulsive

adj.   showing behaviour in which you do things suddenly without any planning and without considering the effects they may have
衝動的

▪ Don't be so impulsive - think before you act.
      不要如此衝動——要三思而後行。

resilient

adj.   able to quickly return to a previous good condition
有彈性的；能復原的；有復原力的；適應性強的

▪ This rubber ball is very resilient and immediately springs back into shape.
      這種橡膠球很有彈性，可以立即恢復原來的形狀。

exquisite

adj.   very beautiful and delicate
精美的；精緻的；優雅的

▪ She has exquisite taste.
      她品味高雅。

inevitable

adj.   certain to happen and unable to be avoided or prevented
不可避免的；必然發生的

▪ The accident was the inevitable outcome of carelessness.
      這次意外是粗心導致的必然結果。

tenacious

adj.   holding tightly onto something, or keeping an opinion in a determined way
緊握的；頑固的；固執的

▪ There has been tenacious local opposition to the new airport.
      當地人一直堅決反對建這個新機場。

peculiar

adj.   unusual and strange, sometimes in an unpleasant way
奇怪的，古怪的

▪ She has the most peculiar ideas.
      她的想法非常古怪。

versatile

adj.   able to change easily from one activity to another or able to be used for many different purposes
多種用途的；萬用的；多才多藝的

▪ He's a very versatile young actor who's as happy in highbrow dramas as he is in TV comedies.
      他是一個非常多才多藝的年輕演員，無論是演高雅電影還是電視喜劇都一樣得心應手。

ubiquitous

adj.   seeming to be everywhere
普遍存在的，似乎無處不在的

▪ Leather is very much in fashion this season, as is the ubiquitous denim.
      皮裝在這個季節非常流行，當然牛仔服也似乎無處不在。

ephemeral

adj.   lasting for only a short time
短暫的，極短的；轉瞬即逝的

▪ Fame in the world of rock and pop is largely ephemeral.
      在搖滾和流行音樂界，名聲大多轉瞬即逝。

serendipity

n.   the fact of finding interesting or valuable things by chance
機緣，幸運

▪ I found the perfect birthday gift for my friend by serendipity while browsing through a vintage store.
      我在一家古董店逛的時候，意外地找到了一份完美的生日禮物。

mellifluous

adj.   having a pleasant and flowing sound
聲音甜美的；圓潤流暢的

▪ the mellifluous sound of the cello
      大提琴甜美圓潤的樂音

idiosyncrasy

n.   a strange or unusual habit, way of behaving, or feature that someone or something has
癖好，怪癖；特徵

▪ She often cracks her knuckles when she's speaking - it's one of her little idiosyncrasies.
      她講話時常常將指節捏得哢哢作響，這是她的一個小怪癖。

pernicious

adj.   having a very harmful effect or influence
極其有害的；惡性的；惡劣的

▪ The cuts in government funding have had a pernicious effect on local health services.
      政府經費的縮減對地方醫衛服務產生了極為惡劣的影響。

esoteric

adj.   very unusual and understood or liked by only a small number of people, especially those with special knowledge
極不尋常的；只有少數人才懂的；限於小圈子的

▪ He has an esoteric collection of old toys and games.
      他收藏了一批極罕見的老玩具和遊戲。

sagacious

adj.   having or showing understanding and the ability to make good judgments
聰慧的，洞察事理的； 睿智的

▪ He is a wise and sagacious leader who always makes wise decisions.
      他是一位聰明而富有智慧的領導者，總是能夠做出明智的決策。

vicarious

adj.   experienced as a result of watching, listening to, or reading about the activities of other people, rather than by doing the activities yourself
間接感受到的；間接獲得的

▪ She took a vicarious pleasure in her friend's achievements.
      她為朋友的成就感到喜悅。

ostentatious

adj.   too obviously showing your money, possessions, or power, in an attempt to make other people notice and admire you
鋪張的，擺闊的；炫耀的，賣弄的；招搖的

▪ They criticized the ostentatious lifestyle of their leaders
      他們批評領導們鋪張的生活方式。
